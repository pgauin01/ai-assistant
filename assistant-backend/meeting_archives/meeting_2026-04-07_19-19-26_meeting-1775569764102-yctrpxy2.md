I built an enterprise‑grade Retrieval‑Augmented Generation pipeline that indexes and queries complex academic documents. The stack combined Python, LangChain, Google Gemini 1.5 Flash, FAISS for dense retrieval, BM25 for sparse keyword search, HuggingFace models like all‑MiniLM and the BGE‑Reranker, and UnstructuredIO for advanced PDF layout parsing. The biggest hurdle was eliminating LLM hallucinations caused by noisy, overflow‑rich context and ensuring exact‑match keyword retrieval. To solve it I engineered a two‑stage retrieval: a hybrid FAISS + BM25 ensemble followed by a cross‑encoder reranker that trimmed the top ten candidates down to the three most relevant chunks. I also fed the refined context into Gemini 1.5 Flash at temperature 0.0 with a strict anti‑hallucination prompt. The result was near‑zero hallucination rates and a reliable, fact‑grounded answer system that can handle both conceptual queries and precise keyword lookups across thousands of academic files.
