A large language model is usually defined by its parameter count—billions of parameters and a massive training corpus—while a small model has millions or fewer. Gemini, for example, runs at roughly 2.5 billion parameters, giving it richer context and better reasoning. The main difference is that larger models capture more nuanced patterns and handle longer prompts, but they cost more compute and memory. Small models are faster and cheaper but trade off depth of understanding and generation quality.
