I built an enterprise‑grade Retrieval‑Augmented Generation pipeline that indexes and queries complex academic documents. The system is powered by Python, LangChain, Google Gemini 1.5 Flash, FAISS for dense retrieval, BM25 for sparse keyword search, HuggingFace models like all‑MiniLM and the BGE cross‑encoder reranker, and UnstructuredIO for advanced PDF layout parsing. The biggest hurdle was preventing LLM hallucinations caused by noisy, overlapping context while also handling exact‑keyword matching for things like part numbers. To solve that I designed a two‑stage retrieval: a hybrid FAISS‑BM25 ensemble followed by a cross‑encoder reranker that trims the top ten candidates down to the three most relevant chunks. I also set Gemini to temperature 0.0 with a strict anti‑hallucination prompt so it only answers when the context is solid. The result was a near‑zero hallucination rate and highly accurate answers for both conceptual queries and precise keyword lookups across thousands of multi‑column PDFs.
