### 1. The Interviewer's Question
Describe a situation where you had to make a strict trade-off between performance and cost when designing a cloud architecture.

### 2. The True Intent
This is a **behavioral question disguised as a system design probe**. The interviewer wants to see: (1) your ability to quantify trade-offs with real metrics, (2) evidence of cost-conscious engineering maturity, and (3) how you justify architectural decisions to stakeholders. The word "strict" signals they want a scenario where you had to sacrifice something meaningful, not a win-win optimization.

### 3. Deep Dive & Strategy
**The HustleBot Lambda Decision:** For HustleBot's job scraping pipeline, I faced a binary choice between AWS Lambda (serverless) and EC2 (always-on compute). **The Performance Sacrifice:** Lambda introduced 2-3 second cold starts when the function hadn't been invoked recently, which would delay the initial scrape request. EC2 would have provided sub-100ms response times consistently. **The Cost Justification:** However, HustleBot's usage pattern was bursty—users would trigger scrapes sporadically throughout the day, not continuously. Running a t3.medium EC2 instance 24/7 would cost ~$30/month in compute alone, plus EBS storage. Lambda's pay-per-execution model (charged only during active scraping) reduced monthly costs to ~$8-10. **The Mitigation Strategy:** I optimized the Lambda deployment package to under 50MB and implemented a CloudWatch Events rule to "warm" the function every 5 minutes during peak hours (9 AM - 6 PM), reducing cold start frequency by 80% while still maintaining the cost advantage. **The Outcome:** The 2-3 second latency was acceptable because job discovery is an asynchronous workflow—users weren't waiting in real-time for results. The 70% cost reduction allowed me to allocate budget toward Playwright browser automation licenses instead.
