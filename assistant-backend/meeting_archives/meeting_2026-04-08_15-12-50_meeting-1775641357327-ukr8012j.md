I built an enterprise‑grade Retrieval‑Augmented Generation pipeline that indexes and queries complex academic documents. The stack combines Python, LangChain, Google Gemini 1.5 Flash, FAISS for dense retrieval, BM25 for sparse keyword search, HuggingFace models like all‑MiniLM and BGE‑Reranker, and UnstructuredIO for advanced PDF layout parsing. The biggest hurdle was eliminating hallucinations caused by noisy, oversized context while also handling exact‑keyword matching for things like part numbers. To solve it I created a two‑stage retrieval: a hybrid FAISS + BM25 ensemble followed by a cross‑encoder reranker that trims the top ten candidates down to the three most relevant chunks. I also forced Gemini to run at temperature 0.0 with a strict anti‑hallucination prompt so it only answers when the context is solid. The result was a near‑zero hallucination system that reliably returns factual answers for both conceptual queries and precise keyword lookups across thousands of academic PDFs.
