I built an enterprise‑grade Retrieval‑Augmented Generation pipeline that indexes and queries complex academic documents. The stack combines Python, LangChain, Google Gemini 1.5 Flash, FAISS for dense retrieval, BM25 for sparse keyword search, HuggingFace models like all‑MiniLM and the BGE‑Reranker, and UnstructuredIO for advanced PDF layout parsing. The biggest hurdle was eliminating LLM hallucinations caused by noisy, oversized context and the mismatch between semantic and exact‑keyword retrieval. To solve it I created a two‑stage retrieval system: a hybrid FAISS + BM25 ensemble followed by a cross‑encoder reranker that trims the top ten candidates down to the three most relevant chunks. I also enforced deterministic generation with Gemini at temperature 0.0 and a strict anti‑hallucination prompt. The result was near‑zero hallucinations and highly accurate answers across both conceptual and exact‑match queries, making the prototype viable for mission‑critical enterprise use.
