### 3. Technical Deep Dive
**Immediate Invocation vs. Deferred Execution.** Both `call` and `apply` execute the target function immediately upon being called, whereas `bind` creates a new bound function with a fixed `this` context that can be executed at any point in the future.

**Argument Handling in Call.** The `call` method accepts arguments individually, passed as a comma-separated list following the `this` context argument.

**Argument Handling in Apply.** The `apply` method is designed for flexibility, accepting arguments as a single array or array-like object, making it ideal for passing dynamic lists of parameters to a function.
